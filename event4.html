On my Birthday...
Nelson Mandela Sentenced to Prison

Nelson Mandela was sentenced to prison on June 12, 1964, after being convicted of sabotage and other charges during his fight against apartheid in South Africa. He, along with several others, was found guilty for his involvement in planning acts of resistance against the oppressive government. Mandela was sentenced to life in prison and would spend 27 years behind bars, much of that time on Robben Island. His imprisonment became a global symbol of resistance to racial injustice, and his eventual release in 1990 marked a turning point in South Africa's struggle for freedom.
